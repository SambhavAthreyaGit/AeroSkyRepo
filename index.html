The Aerosky Process

Capturing Carbon Emissions

Using air contacters placed on the ground, we are able to capture carbon dioxide burned by factories, cars, and more using carbon capture and absorb and preserve them with sorbents. This can help mitigate the effects of climate change.

Forming Calcium Carbonate

Using the CO2 captured, we are able to react it with hydroxide in the air contractor to form carbonate. Then we can react the carbonate with calcium hydroxide in a pellet reactor forming calcium carbonate in the form of pellets.

Releasing Produced Aerosols

The pellets are then released from aircraft into the stratosphere. We are then able to use calcium carbonate in the stratosphere and aerosols in solar geoengineering to reflect the sun’s rays allowing us to rapidly lower global temperature and precipitation and other linked problems.

Cloud Seeding

A solution containing a small amount of silver iodide is released from aircrafts. These salt particles act as a core that draws water vapor within the cloud toward them that allowing more rainfall in developing countries that will lose more precipitation in solar geoengineering.

Capturing our Way into the Future

The Problem

As many of you know from the recent mass attraction brought to this issue, climate change is deteriorating our planet. Global temperatures increase levels from 1901/2022 and 1975/2022 have almost tripled, Global precipitation has also risen rapidly, which has been the root cause to the problems that we face today. Habitats are dying out; 17 million animals have died due to climate change. The more considerable relevance is the food shortages, ocean acidification, wildfires, and other natural disasters we face today. In fact, the sheer scale of the disaster facing the planet shocked those involved in the research. They estimate that more than 1 million species will be lost by 2050 and who knows, the rate we are going at, the human race might be next.

Our Solution

Global warming is the most prominent problem that humanity has faced, and it will require the most prominent solution: Aerosky. Our solution involves capturing carbon dioxide in the atmosphere using air contractors and absorbing them with sorbents. Then we can use part of the Fischer-touch process to make calcium carbonate. This solution process a new and cheap form of carbon by taking out one of the most expensive parts of the process, the calciner. We can then use the calcium carbonate as an aerosol in the sky to limit the root cause of climate change, the sun’s rays. The tiny reflective particle then reflects the sun’s rays without using the conventional sulfur dioxide aerosols, which pose enough problems by themselves.

The Outcomes

With Aerosky we can rapidly achieve climate change goals that would have taken decades to achieve with other solutions. If our solution is implemented, 90% of man-made carbon could be reduced, global temperature could be reduced by 1.5° out of the 2° that climate change has raised it by, and global precipitation could be reduced by 4.5%. This would cause fewer species to go extinct and less deforestation and countless other climate change impacts. With solar geoengineering, we could act as a shield over earth battling against its harmful impacts until carbon capture and renewable energies become sufficient allowing us to solve climate change.

Our Future

The Future of Aerosky

Invented in the middle of the 20th century, solar geoengineering is still in its infancy stages and requires much more research for it to be a reality. On the other hand, carbon capture is more developed and defined with many more applications but is still only implemented at low scales due to its price. However, the connection between these two industries would be industry-breaking. This idea has the potential to change the world, efficiently remove carbon out of the air, and create a shield on the earth, protecting us from harsh impacts while only using tiny reflective droplets.

With a variant of the Fischer-Tropsch process and several Stratospheric Aerosol Injection researchers, we could make the solution that we have all dreamt of in as little time as 5-10 years. Putting side effects to rest, such as lowering the price using this variant carbon process and solving the lack of precipitation using cloud seeding using silver iodide, this solution provides minor proven implications with a much higher reward.

RESEARCH COMPLETED (2025)

Research needs to be completed on the effects and uses of Calcium Carbonate as an aerosol and what impacts on the atmosphere it can potentially have. The perfect ratio of Calcium Carbonate also needs to be found that can be most reflective and efficient but does not reflect too many sun’s rays causing a global issue.

AIR CONTACTER SITE MANUFACTURED (2027)

After research has been completed on calcium carbonate as an aerosol, we need to set up the air contacter site in a rural area. Costing around $10,000 per site for just the air contacters, it would take 1-2 years for these sites to be set up.

DEVELOPING PLANES (2029)

Once we have the contactor sites manufactured, we can set up the solar geoengineering aircraft to release aerosols into the stratosphere. We would only need to manufacture three aircraft per 1-2 years per region.

STARTING THE CARBON CAPTURE PROCESS (2023)

Most sites have been fully developed and implemented, which allows us to start implementing the Aerosky process. We can capture the carbon efficiently and rapidly generate calcium carbonate aerosols.

RELEASING AEROSOLS INTO THE STRATOSPHERE

Now we can accomplish everything from the planes and the sites and have shown how we can efficiently generate calcium carbonate aerosols. We can now use all of these sites and equipment to release the aerosols into the stratosphere so that we can reflect the sun’s rays.
